Blink Privacy Policy

Last updated: February 2026

Blink is a Chrome extension that helps you track LinkedIn follow-ups. We are committed to protecting your privacy. This policy explains what data Blink collects, how it is stored, and how it is used.

1. Data We Collect

Blink collects only the data you explicitly choose to save:

  • LinkedIn profile URL (used as a unique identifier)
  • Name, job title, and company (read from the LinkedIn profile page)
  • Follow-up status, follow-up date, and personal notes you enter

2. How Data Is Stored

All data is stored locally on your device using chrome.storage.local. No data is ever sent to any external server, database, or third party. Blink has no backend.

3. Data Sharing

We do not share, sell, or transmit your data to anyone. Your data never leaves your browser.

4. Permissions

Blink requests the following Chrome permissions:

  • storage – to save your contacts locally
  • alarms – to trigger daily overdue reminders
  • activeTab / linkedin.com host permission – to read LinkedIn profile pages when you are on them

No permission is used for any purpose beyond the features described above.

5. Deleting Your Data

You can delete individual contacts from the Blink popup at any time. To remove all data, simply uninstall the extension — this permanently deletes all locally stored data.

6. Children's Privacy

Blink is not directed at children under 13 and does not knowingly collect data from them.
